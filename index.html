Phone Prototype Showcase

According to ExplodingTopics.com, the phone is the most used device to connect a user to a website: in fact over 63% of website veiwers are on using a phone. Keeping this in mind the phone was the biggest push in development when it comes to the CAM UX showcase: the paralax effect makes the viewer feel immersed and the biggest design goal was to make everything make sense in a phone aspect ratio.

Research

A study that was done by Garett, Renne et al. "A Literature Review: Website Design fo User Engagement" argues that an emphisis on graphical representation is to be more important elements that brought user engagement to a website. User engagment and the website being memoriable is linked to how visually engaging the website is.


Another study that was published by the International Jounral of Human-Computer Interaction found that users form an aesthetic impression of a website within the first second of seeing said website. Websites that have a high visual importance with moderate xomplexity were perveiced as more appealing: this appealing nature leads to more engagement and an higher interaction rate with said website. These quick evaluations of the uswers remaind over time: this highlights the importance of the initial impression of the website and how its linked to user engagment.


Hitit University used an eye-tracker to asses a websites performance. The study showed that designs changes the users engagmenet and attention shown to the website. The engagmenet metrics such as the first view, a reviewm average focus time, and number of views are heavily affected by the visual appeal of said website. This study highlights the impact of design elements and its direct link to user interaction and engagment.
